Note: this is not about semantic validation. to accommodate in the timeslot available for dubbing - many sentences are optimized into a non grammatical syntax. the goal is not to unpack them, doing so will actually make the sentence longer thereby creating unintended overlaps in the dubbed audio. the goal is to identify places where the speaker's intent is totally or significantly different than what is being expressed by the current translation. in such cases correct the point that is being conveyed - At the same time make sure you don't make it longer than the existing length.

### **PART 6: SYNTAX OF REPORTED SPEECH (BRINGING THE VERB TO THE END)**

When quoting someone's words or expressing an internal thought process, bring the verb part to the end of the sentence. Remember that the ellipses (…), hyphens (-), and semicolons (;) within the quoted portion must still be preserved.

Crucial Exception (Direct Quotes vs. Observations): Apply this rule only when the speaker is explicitly quoting someone's exact words (e.g., "He said, '...'") or explicitly stating a specific internal monologue (e.g., "I thought to myself, '...'"). Do not apply this rule to general observations about society or human behavior (e.g., "People think that..."). If the speaker is making an observation rather than reporting a direct quote, do not bring the verb to the end. Maintain the original sequence of the thought units.

**Example 1:**

* Original: ওঁরা <T3>মনে করেন<T3> এখন আধ্যাত্মিকতা মানে আপনাকে হিমালয়ে যেতে হবে বা কাশীতে যেতে হবে... অথবা সব ছেড়ে দিতে হবে - এই বলে
* Optimized: ওঁরা “এখন আধ্যাত্মিকতা মানে আপনাকে হিমালয়ে যেতে হবে বা কাশীতে যেতে হবে... অথবা সব ছেড়ে দিতে হবে” এই বলে <T3>মনে করেন<T3>
* Description: Structure is: Subject, then comma, then the quote, then the verb (saying/thinking), then punctuation.

**Example 2:**

* Original: প্রত্যেকেই <T3>জিজ্ঞেস করছেন<T3>, "সদ্‌গুরু, আপনি এর জন্য এত মাথা ঘামাচ্ছেন কেন?"
* Optimized: প্রত্যেকেই, "সদ্‌গুরু, আপনি এর জন্য এত মাথা ঘামাচ্ছেন কেন?" - এই বলে <T3>জিজ্ঞেস করছেন<T3>।

**Example 3:**

* Original: <T3>আমি বললাম<T3>, ওঁদের যদি খাওয়ার জলই না থাকে... তাহলে আমি ওঁদের ধ্যান করতে কীভাবে বলব?
* Optimized: <T3>আমি<T3>, “ওঁদের যদি খাওয়ার জলই না থাকে... তাহলে আমি ওঁদের ধ্যান করতে কীভাবে বলব?” <T3>বললাম<T3>
* Description: The subject who spoke and the verb that conveys speaking were in one word. We separated them: subject before the quote, verb after the quote.

**Example 4:**

* Original: মানুষ আমাকে <T3>জিজ্ঞেস করতেই থাকেন<T3>, "সদ্‌গুরু, এই দেশে আধ্যাত্মিক প্রক্রিয়া পুনরুদ্ধারের পথ কী?"
* Optimized: মানুষ আমাকে <T3>এভাবেই<T3> জিজ্ঞেস করতে থাকেন, "সদ্‌গুরু, এই দেশে আধ্যাত্মিক প্রক্রিয়া পুনরুদ্ধারের পথ কী?" <T3>বলেন।<T3>
* Description: The subject part is long, and bringing the whole phrase to the end may cause sync loss while dubbing. We added the verb that conveys asking again at the end to make it natural spoken Bengali, and added the word 'এভাবেই' as a discourse-bridging adverb.

### **Part 7: Anchoring between the subparts of a complex sentence.

idea chunks

pause-driven fragments

 Translation rule:

 Translate by thought, not by grammar.

Sadhguru uses silence as punctuation.

He often pauses:

before the punchline

after a deep question

before contradiction (“No…”)

 Translation rule:

 Preserve pause points even if grammar feels “broken”.



 C. Question → Silence → Answer pattern

He frequently does:

Ask a simple question

Pause

Turn the question upside down

Example:

“Do you want peace?

Or do you want to control everything?”

 Translation rule:

 Keep questions short and spoken, not literary.



Analogy:

Let me explain the target with an analogy.

Let's take a human body.

It is made of various body parts like - face, hands, torso, legs

and now each body part is made of various subparts. Example: face has eyes, nose, mouth, ears.

And each sub part has various internal parts. Example: eye has eyeball, eyelids.

And each internal part has building parts. Example: eyeball has white eyeball and black eyeball.

Situation:
As it is already told, the talk relies on "cumulative syntax" (stacking ideas, pause-driven fragments) rather than grammatically complete sentences.
And the segments in translation are placed literally in the same order. This leads to broken flow.

But at the same time keep the idea delivery in the same sequence.

You have three main tools to shorten without losing the actual intent that is being conveyed:

Sandhi (Vowel Merging / সন্ধি): Merge vowels and adjacent sounds where Bengali naturally permits it. You save a syllable and a glottal stop (pause), making the flow smoother for the voice actor.
Samasa (Compound Nouns / সমাস): Instead of "প্রাণের সংকেত" (The signs of life), you use "প্রাণ-সংকেত". It sounds tighter and cuts the length significantly.
Verb Fusing / Cholito Contractions: Use spoken forms — "করিতেছি" → "করছি"; "যাইতেছি" → "যাচ্ছি"; "হইয়াছে" → "হয়েছে". It turns a concept into a single action.
