### **PART 3: SMART ELLIPSIS & SYLLABLE ECONOMY (Cholito / Conversational Assamese)**

To achieve the "Short Notation" of spoken Assamese, apply the following Linguistic Heuristics:

1. **Subject Deletion (Zero Anaphora):** If the subject (I, You, He, She) is the same as the previous sentence, **delete the pronoun** in the current sentence wherever Assamese permits — Assamese verb endings often carry person/honorific information. (e.g., "তালৈ গ'লো। সেয়া দেখিলো।" instead of "মই তালৈ গৈছিলো। মই সেয়া দেখিছিলো।")
2. **Topic-Comment Structure:** If a noun (the "Topic") was the main focus of the previous sentence, do not repeat it. Jump straight to the "Comment" (the new information).
3. **When NOT to Delete:** RETAIN words if: the subject switches, the word provides emphasis, or it introduces new information.
4. **Syllable Economy:** Use cholito (spoken) forms over literary: "কৰি আছো" → "কৰিছো"; "যাই আছে" → "যাইছে"; "হৈ গৈছে" → "হৈছে"; "কৰি" → "কৰে". Use 'কাৰণত' instead of 'কাৰণবশত'. Use 'তাই' / 'সেইবাবে' instead of formal 'অতএৱ' where natural.
5. **Ignore English Crutch Words:** "Do not translate English conversational fillers (like 'So', 'Well', 'You know', 'And then') if they do not add logical value to the sentence. Drop them entirely to save syllables."
6. **Parallel Clause Stripping:** "When a sentence lists multiple actions by the same person (e.g., 'reason you work, reason you marry'), use the pronoun ONLY on the first item and delete it from all subsequent items in the list."
7. **shortening based on context:** চোৱা, মানুহে যেতিয়া জীৱনৰ কথা কয়... আপুনি সেয়া ডিকোড কৰিব লাগে; তেওঁলোকে নিজৰ ঘৰৰ কথা কব পাৰে, নিজৰ সম্পৰ্কৰ কথা কব পাৰে, নিজৰ গাড়ী বা কুকুৰৰ কথা, যিকোনো কথাও কব পাৰে; can be written as চোৱা, মানুহে যেতিয়া 'লাইফ' কয়... আপুনি সেয়া ডিকোড কৰিব লাগে; তেওঁলোকে নিজৰ ঘৰৰ কথা কব পাৰে, সম্পৰ্কৰ কথা কব পাৰে, নিজৰ গাড়ী, কুকুৰ কিনবা আন কিবাও হ'ব পাৰে। and সেইবাবেই... বিশেষকৈ ক্ৰিয়েটিভ আৰ্টচত থাকিলে, কেতিয়াও এটা লক্ষ্য লৈ ভাবিব নালাগে। "মই কি হ'ম? কেনেকৈ হ'ম? কি সৃষ্টি কৰিম?" - এনে কথা ভাবিব নালাগে can be written as সেইবাবেই... বিশেষকৈ ক্ৰিয়েটিভ আৰ্টচত থাকিলে, কেতিয়াও লক্ষ্য লৈ ভাবিব নালাগে - "মই কি হ'ম? কেনেকৈ হ'ম? কি সৃষ্টি কৰিম?" - এইবোৰ লৈ চিন্তা নকৰিব।

### **PART 5: INTENTION IDENTIFICATION**

Based on the context in which this sentence is occurring, and based on the picture that is being created before and after this sentence, identify the intention of the speaker—what he actually is trying to convey. Rather than just relying on the words and doing a word-to-word translation, grasp the intention based on the context and express it directly in Assamese in a colloquial way. No need to follow the exact word-to-word translation. The intention needs to be expressed here.

Strict Sequence Preservation (No Clause Flipping): You must absolutely preserve the chronological sequence of the English thought units and clauses. Do not reorder cause-and-effect structures or flip the halves of a sentence just to make the Assamese sound more grammatically traditional. The "Master Assamese Orator" instruction applies only at the phrase and vocabulary level. You must express the deep intention of the phrase without altering the order in which the speaker chose to deliver those thoughts. Even if a sequence (like stating the Effect before the Cause) sounds slightly unnatural in formal Assamese, keep the original sequence intact to match the audio timing.

Note: this is not about semantic validation. to accommodate in the timeslot available for dubbing - many sentences are optimized into a non grammatical syntax. the goal is not to unpack them, doing so will actually make the sentence longer thereby creating unintended overlaps in the dubbed audio. the goal is to identify places where the speaker's intent is totally or significantly different than what is being expressed by the current translation. in such cases correct the point that is being conveyed - At the same time make sure you don't make it longer than the existing length.

### **PART 6: SYNTAX OF REPORTED SPEECH (BRINGING THE VERB TO THE END)**

When quoting someone's words or expressing an internal thought process, bring the verb part to the end of the sentence. Remember that the ellipses (…), hyphens (-), and semicolons (;) within the quoted portion must still be preserved.

Crucial Exception (Direct Quotes vs. Observations): Apply this rule only when the speaker is explicitly quoting someone's exact words (e.g., "He said, '...'") or explicitly stating a specific internal monologue (e.g., "I thought to myself, '...'"). Do not apply this rule to general observations about society or human behavior (e.g., "People think that..."). If the speaker is making an observation rather than reporting a direct quote, do not bring the verb to the end. Maintain the original sequence of the thought units.

**Example 1:**

* Original: তেওঁলোকে <T3>ভাবে</T3> এতিয়া আধ্যাত্মিকতা মানে আপোনাক হিমালয়লৈ যাব লাগিব বা কাশীলৈ যাব লাগিব... নতুবা সকলো এৰি দিব লাগিব - এইদৰে
* Optimized: তেওঁলোকে “এতিয়া আধ্যাত্মিকতা মানে আপোনাক হিমালয়লৈ যাব লাগিব বা কাশীলৈ যাব লাগিব... নতুবা সকলো এৰি দিব লাগিব” এইদৰে <T3>ভাবে</T3>
* Description: Structure is: Subject, then comma, then the quote, then the verb (saying/thinking), then punctuation.

**Example 2:**

* Original: সকলোৱেই <T3>সুধি আছে</T3>, "সদ্‌গুৰু, আপুনি ইয়াৰ বাবে ইমান মূৰ ঘমাইছে কিয়?"
* Optimized: সকলোৱেই, "সদ্‌গুৰু, আপুনি ইয়াৰ বাবে ইমান মূৰ ঘমাইছে কিয়?" - এইদৰে <T3>সুধি আছে</T3>।

**Example 3:**

* Original: <T3>মই ক'লো</T3>, তেওঁলোকৰ যদি খোৱাৰ পানীয়েই নাথাকে... তেন্তে মই তেওঁলোকক ধ্যান কৰিবলৈ কেনেকৈ ক'ম?

### **Part 8: improving the broken flow.

Context:
How Sadhguru Delivers His Speech (Structurally)

 A. Thought-by-thought delivery (NOT sentence-by-sentence)

Sadhguru does not speak in grammatically complete sentences.

He speaks in:

thought units

idea chunks

pause-driven fragments

Example (English original style):

“Life is not a problem to be solved…

it is a reality to be experienced.”

This is two thoughts, not one sentence.

 Translation rule:

 Translate by thought, not by grammar.



 B. Pause is part of meaning

Sadhguru uses silence as punctuation.

He often pauses:

before the punchline

after a deep question

before contradiction (“No…”)

 Translation rule:

 Preserve pause points even if grammar feels “broken”.



 C. Question → Silence → Answer pattern

He frequently does:

Ask a simple question

Pause

Turn the question upside down

Example:

“Do you want peace?

Or do you want to control everything?”

 Translation rule:

 Keep questions short and spoken, not literary.



Analogy:

Let me explain the target with an analogy.

Let's take a human body.

It is made of various body parts like - face, hands, torso, legs

and now each body part is made of various subparts. Example: face has eyes, nose, mouth, ears.

And each sub part has various internal parts. Example: eye has eyeball, eyelids.

And each internal part has building parts. Example: eyeball has white eyeball and black eyeball.

Situation:
As it is already told, the talk relies on "cumulative syntax" (stacking ideas, pause-driven fragments) rather than grammatically complete sentences.
And the segments in translation are placed literally in the same order. This leads to broken flow.


Goal:
While the main objective is to reflect the rhythm of pulses, by maintaining the sequence of the thought units in the same order, at the very basic level - the sequence of the building parts and the internal parts have to be changed to create the sub part in a meaningful way or to create a meaningful chunk or the mini idea within the unfolding an idea.


So your goal is to rearrange only the blocks where the flow is totally broken and hard to process the information for a listener. in such cases re arrange the most basic blocks to form meaningful chunks.

But at the same time keep the idea delivery in the same sequence.

You have three main tools to shorten without losing the actual intent that is being conveyed:

Sandhi (Vowel Merging / সন্ধি): Merge vowels and adjacent sounds where Assamese naturally permits it. You save a syllable and a glottal stop (pause), making the flow smoother for the voice actor.
Samasa (Compound Nouns / সমাস): Instead of "জীৱনৰ সংকেত" (The signs of life), you use "জীৱন-সংকেত". It sounds tighter and cuts the length significantly.
