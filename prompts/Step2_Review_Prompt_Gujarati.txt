### **PART 3: SMART ELLIPSIS & SYLLABLE ECONOMY (Cholito / Conversational Gujarati)**

To achieve the "Short Notation" of spoken Gujarati, apply the following Linguistic Heuristics:

1. **Subject Deletion (Zero Anaphora):** If the subject (I, You, He, She) is the same as the previous sentence, **delete the pronoun** in the current sentence wherever Gujarati permits — Gujarati verb endings often carry person/honorific information. (e.g., "ત્યાં ગયો. એ જોયું." instead of "હું ત્યાં ગયો હતો. મેં એ જોયું હતું.")
2. **Topic-Comment Structure:** If a noun (the "Topic") was the main focus of the previous sentence, do not repeat it. Jump straight to the "Comment" (the new information).
3. **When NOT to Delete:** RETAIN words if: the subject switches, the word provides emphasis, or it introduces new information.
4. **Syllable Economy:** Use bolchal (spoken) forms over literary forms: "કરી રહ્યો છું" → "કરું છું"; "જઈ રહ્યા છો" → "જઈ રો"; "થઈ ગયું છે" → "થઈ ગ્યું". Use 'કારણ' instead of 'કારણવશ'. Use 'એટલે' / 'તેથી' instead of formal 'અતઃ' where natural.
5. **Ignore English Crutch Words:** "Do not translate English conversational fillers (like 'So', 'Well', 'You know', 'And then') if they do not add logical value to the sentence. Drop them entirely to save syllables."
6. **Parallel Clause Stripping:** "When a sentence lists multiple actions by the same person (e.g., 'reason you work, reason you marry'), use the pronoun ONLY on the first item and delete it from all subsequent items in the list."
7. **shortening based on context:** જુઓ, જ્યારે માણસ જીવનની વાત કરે... તમારે એ ડિકોડ કરવું પડે; એ પોતાના ઘરની વાત કરી શકે, પોતાના સંબંધોની વાત કરી શકે, પોતાની ગાડી કે કૂતરાની, ગમે તે વાત કરી શકે; can be written as જુઓ, જ્યારે માણસ 'જીવન' કહે... તમારે એ ડિકોડ કરવું પડે; એ ઘરની વાત કરી શકે, સંબંધોની, ગાડી, કૂતરો - કઈ પણ હોઈ શકે. and એટલે... ખાસ કરીને ક્રિએટિવ આર્ટ્સમાં હો તો, ક્યારેય ધ્યેય વિશે વિચારો નહીં - "હું શું બનીશ? કેવો બનીશ? શું સર્જીશ?" - આ બધું વિચારો નહીં.

Strict Sequence Preservation (No Clause Flipping): You must absolutely preserve the chronological sequence of the English thought units and clauses. Do not reorder cause-and-effect structures or flip the halves of a sentence just to make the Gujarati sound more grammatically traditional. The "Master Gujarati Orator" instruction applies only at the phrase and vocabulary level. You must express the deep intention of the phrase without altering the order in which the speaker chose to deliver those thoughts. Even if a sequence (like stating the Effect before the Cause) sounds slightly unnatural in formal Gujarati, keep the original sequence intact to match the audio timing.

Note: this is not about semantic validation. to accommodate in the timeslot available for dubbing - many sentences are optimized into a non grammatical syntax. the goal is not to unpack them, doing so will actually make the sentence longer thereby creating unintended overlaps in the dubbed audio. the goal is to identify places where the speaker's intent is totally or significantly different than what is being expressed by the current translation. in such cases correct the point that is being conveyed - At the same time make sure you don't make it longer than the existing length.

### **PART 6: SYNTAX OF REPORTED SPEECH (BRINGING THE VERB TO THE END)**

When quoting someone's words or expressing an internal thought process, bring the verb part to the end of the sentence. Remember that the ellipses (…), hyphens (-), and semicolons (;) within the quoted portion must still be preserved.

Crucial Exception (Direct Quotes vs. Observations): Apply this rule only when the speaker is explicitly quoting someone's exact words (e.g., "He said, '...'") or explicitly stating a specific internal monologue (e.g., "I thought to myself, '...'"). Do not apply this rule to general observations about society or human behavior (e.g., "People think that..."). If the speaker is making an observation rather than reporting a direct quote, do not bring the verb to the end. Maintain the original sequence of the thought units.

**Example 1:**

* Original: તેઓ <T3>માને</T3> કે હવે આધ્યાત્મિકતા એ તમારે હિમાલય જવું પડે અથવા કાશી જવું પડે... અથવા બધું છોડી દેવું - એ ભાવ
* Optimized: તેઓ “હવે આધ્યાત્મિકતા એ તમારે હિમાલય જવું પડે અથવા કાશી જવું પડે... અથવા બધું છોડી દેવું” - આ ભાવ <T3>માને</T3>
* Description: Structure is: Subject, then comma, then the quote, then the verb (saying/thinking), then punctuation.

**Example 2:**

* Original: બધા <T3>પૂછી રહ્યા</T3>, "સદ્‌ગુરુ, તમ આ માટે આટલા કેમ ચિંતિત?"
* Optimized: બધા, "સદ્‌ગુરુ, તમ આ માટે આટલા કેમ ચિંતિત?" - આ ભાવ <T3>પૂછી રહ્યા</T3>.

**Example 3:**

* Original: <T3>મેં કહ્યું</T3>, જો તેઓ પાસ પીવાનું પાણી ન હોય... તો હું તેઓને ધ્યાન કરવા કઈ રીતે કહું?
* Optimized: <T3>મેં</T3>, “જો તેઓ પાસ પીવાનું પાણી ન હોય... તો હું તેઓને ધ્યાન કરવા કઈ રીતે કહું?” <T3>કહ્યું</T3>
* Description: The subject who spoke and the verb that conveys speaking were in one word. We separated them: subject before the quote, verb after the quote.

**Example 4:**

* Original: લોક મને <T3>પૂછ્યા જ કરે</T3>, "સદ્‌ગુરુ, આ દેશમાં આધ્યાત્મિક પ્રક્રિયા ફરી સ્થાપવાનો માર્ગ શો?"

**Main Example 1:**

pause-driven fragments

 Translate by thought, not by grammar.

He often pauses:

before the punchline

after a deep question

before contradiction (“No…”)

 Translation rule:

 Preserve pause points even if grammar feels “broken”.



 C. Question → Silence → Answer pattern

He frequently does:

Ask a simple question

Pause

Turn the question upside down

Example:

“Do you want peace?

Or do you want to control everything?”

 Translation rule:

 Keep questions short and spoken, not literary.



Analogy:

Let me explain the target with an analogy.

Let's take a human body.

It is made of various body parts like - face, hands, torso, legs

And each sub part has various internal parts. Example: eye has eyeball, eyelids.

And each internal part has building parts. Example: eyeball has white eyeball and black eyeball.

But at the same time keep the idea delivery in the same sequence.

You have three main tools to shorten without losing the actual intent that is being conveyed:

Sandhi (Vowel Merging / સ્વર-સંધિ): Merge vowels and adjacent sounds where Gujarati naturally permits it. You save a syllable and a glottal stop (pause), making the flow smoother for the voice actor.
Samasa (Compound Nouns / સમાસ): Instead of "જીવનની નિશાની" (The signs of life), you use "જીવન-નિશાની". It sounds tighter and cuts the length significantly.
Verb Fusing / Cholito Contractions: Use spoken forms — "કરી રહ્યો છું" → "કરું છું"; "જઈ રહ્યા છો" → "જઈ ર્યો"; "થઈ ગયું છે" → "થઈ ગ્યું". It turns a concept into a single action.
