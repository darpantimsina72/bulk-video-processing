### **PART 5: INTENTION IDENTIFICATION**

Based on the context in which this sentence is occurring, and based on the picture that is being created before and after this sentence, identify the intention of the speaker—what he actually is trying to convey. Rather than just relying on the words and doing a word-to-word translation, grasp the intention based on the context and express it directly in Tamil in a colloquial way. No need to follow the exact word-to-word translation. The intention needs to be expressed here.

Strict Sequence Preservation (No Clause Flipping): You must absolutely preserve the chronological sequence of the English thought units and clauses. Do not reorder cause-and-effect structures or flip the halves of a sentence just to make the Tamil sound more grammatically traditional. The "Master Tamil Orator" instruction applies only at the phrase and vocabulary level. You must express the deep intention of the phrase without altering the order in which the speaker chose to deliver those thoughts. Even if a sequence (like stating the Effect before the Cause) sounds slightly unnatural in formal Tamil, keep the original sequence intact to match the audio timing.

Note: this is not about semantic validation. to accommodate in the timeslot available for dubbing - many sentences are optimized into a non grammatical syntax. the goal is not to unpack them, doing so will actually make the sentence longer thereby creating unintended overlaps in the dubbed audio. the goal is to identify places where the speaker's intent is totally or significantly different than what is being expressed by the current translation. in such cases correct the point that is being conveyed - At the same time make sure you don't make it longer than the existing length.

### **PART 6: SYNTAX OF REPORTED SPEECH (BRINGING THE VERB TO THE END)**

When quoting someone's words or expressing an internal thought process, bring the verb part to the end of the sentence. Remember that the ellipses (…), hyphens (-), and semicolons (;) within the quoted portion must still be preserved.

Crucial Exception (Direct Quotes vs. Observations): Apply this rule only when the speaker is explicitly quoting someone's exact words (e.g., "He said, '...'") or explicitly stating a specific internal monologue (e.g., "I thought to myself, '...'"). Do not apply this rule to general observations about society or human behavior (e.g., "People think that..."). If the speaker is making an observation rather than reporting a direct quote, do not bring the verb to the end. Maintain the original sequence of the thought units.

**Example 1:**

* Original: அவங்க <T3>நினைக்கிறாங்க<T3> இப்போ ஆன்மிகம்னா நீங்க இமயமலைக்கு போகணும் கி காசிக்கு போகணும்... இல்லாட்டி எல்லாத்தையும் விட்டுவிடணும்

Sandhi (புணர்ச்சி / Vowel Merging): Merge vowels and adjacent sounds where Tamil naturally permits it (e.g., "அது இருக்கிறது" → "அதிருக்கு"; "செய்கிறேன்" → "செய்றேன்"). You save a syllable and a glottal stop (pause), making the flow smoother for the voice actor.
