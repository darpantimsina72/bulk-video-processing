### **PART 6: SYNTAX OF REPORTED SPEECH (BRINGING THE VERB TO THE END)**

When quoting someone's words or expressing an internal thought process, bring the verb part to the end of the sentence. Remember that the ellipses (…), hyphens (-), and semicolons (;) within the quoted portion must still be preserved.

Crucial Exception (Direct Quotes vs. Observations): Apply this rule only when the speaker is explicitly quoting someone's exact words (e.g., "He said, '...'") or explicitly stating a specific internal monologue (e.g., "I thought to myself, '...'"). Do not apply this rule to general observations about society or human behavior (e.g., "People think that..."). If the speaker is making an observation rather than reporting a direct quote, do not bring the verb to the end. Maintain the original sequence of the thought units.

**Example 1:**

* Original: उहाँहरूले <T3>सोच्नुहुन्छ<T3> अहिले आध्यात्मिकता भनेको तपाईंलाई हिमालय जानु पर्छ वा काशी जानु पर्छ... वा सब छोड्नु पर्छ - भनेर
* Optimized: उहाँहरूले “अहिले आध्यात्मिकता भनेको तपाईंलाई हिमालय जानु पर्छ वा काशी जानु पर्छ... वा सब छोड्नु पर्छ” भनेर <T3>सोच्नुहुन्छ<T3>
* Description: Structure is: Subject, then comma, then the quote, then the verb (saying/thinking), then punctuation.

**Example 2:**

* Original: सबैले <T3>सोधिरहनुभएको छ<T3>, "सद्गुरु, तपाईं यसको लागि यति किन चिन्तित हुनुहुन्छ?"
* Optimized: सबैले, "सद्गुरु, तपाईं यसको लागि यति किन चिन्तित हुनुहुन्छ?" - भनेर <T3>सोधिरहनुभएको छ<T3>।

**Example 3:**

* Original: <T3>मैले भनेँ<T3>, उहाँहरूसँग पिउने पानी नै छैन भने... तब म उहाँहरूलाई ध्यान गर्न कसरी भन्ने?
* Optimized: <T3>मैले<T3>, “उहाँहरूसँग पिउने पानी नै छैन भने... तब म उहाँहरूलाई ध्यान गर्न कसरी भन्ने?” <T3>भनेँ<T3>
* Description: The subject who spoke and the verb that conveys speaking were in one word. We separated them: subject before the quote, verb after the quote.

**Example 4:**

* Original: मानिसहरूले मलाई <T3>सोधिरहनु हुन्छ<T3>, "सद्गुरु, यस देशमा आध्यात्मिक प्रक्रिया पुनर्स्थापना गर्ने बाटो के हो?"
* Optimized: मानिसहरूले मलाई <T3>यसरी नै<T3> सोधिरहनु हुन्छ, "सद्गुरु, यस देशमा आध्यात्मिक प्रक्रिया पुनर्स्थापना गर्ने बाटो के हो?" <T3>भन्छन्।<T3>
* Description: The subject part is long, and bringing the whole phrase to the end may cause sync loss while dubbing. We added the verb that conveys asking again at the end to make it natural spoken Nepali, and added the word 'यसरी नै' as a discourse-bridging adverb.

### **Part 8: improving the broken flow.

Context:
How Sadhguru Delivers His Speech (Structurally)

 A. Thought-by-thought delivery (NOT sentence-by-sentence)

Sadhguru does not speak in grammatically complete sentences.

He speaks in:

thought units

idea chunks

pause-driven fragments

Example (English original style):

“Life is not a problem to be solved…

it is a reality to be experienced.”

This is two thoughts, not one sentence.

 Translation rule:

 Translate by thought, not by grammar.



 B. Pause is part of meaning

Sadhguru uses silence as punctuation.

He often pauses:

before the punchline

after a deep question

before contradiction (“No…”)

 Translation rule:

 Preserve pause points even if grammar feels “broken”.



 C. Question → Silence → Answer pattern

He frequently does:

Ask a simple question

Pause

Turn the question upside down

Example:

“Do you want peace?

Or do you want to control everything?”

 Translation rule:

 Keep questions short and spoken, not literary.



Analogy:

Let me explain the target with an analogy.

Let's take a human body.

It is made of various body parts like - face, hands, torso, legs

and now each body part is made of various subparts. Example: face has eyes, nose, mouth, ears.

And each sub part has various internal parts. Example: eye has eyeball, eyelids.

And each internal part has building parts. Example: eyeball has white eyeball and black eyeball.

Situation:
As it is already told, the talk relies on "cumulative syntax" (stacking ideas, pause-driven fragments) rather than grammatically complete sentences.
And the segments in translation are placed literally in the same order. This leads to broken flow.


Goal:
While the main objective is to reflect the rhythm of pulses, by maintaining the sequence of the thought units in the same order, at the very basic level - the sequence of the building parts and the internal parts have to be changed to create the sub part in a meaningful way or to create a meaningful chunk or the mini idea within the unfolding an idea.


So your goal is to rearrange only the blocks where the flow is totally broken and hard to process the information for a listener. in such cases re arrange the most basic blocks to form meaningful chunks.

But at the same time keep the idea delivery in the same sequence.
